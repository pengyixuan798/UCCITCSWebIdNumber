Dr. Norman Borlaug

The scientist whose work helped feed billions

Why he matters

Norman Borlaug developed high-yielding, disease-resistant wheat varieties and helped launch the Green Revolution, dramatically increasing food production in many developing countries and saving millions from starvation.

Selected milestones

  • 1944
    Started breeding wheat varieties in Mexico.
  • 1960s
    Green Revolution spreads to South Asia — major yield increases in India and Pakistan.
  • 1970
    Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for contributions to world food supply.
"There are no inevitabilities in human affairs — not in war, not in hunger, not in revolution." — Norman Borlaug
